01 — DOMAIN

Research Domain

Literature Survey

Modern soldier support systems increasingly rely on IoT wearables, embedded sensors, and wireless telemetry to improve battlefield awareness. Prior studies show the value of continuous health monitoring, fall detection, and remote situational reporting — but many solutions are short-range, power-intensive, or not designed for harsh field conditions.

Research Gap

Existing systems lack an integrated wearable design combining health monitoring, fall detection, location awareness, and long-range secure transmission in a field-ready format. Most dashboards display raw sensor readings without predictive decision-support for health or link reliability.

Research Problem

Soldiers in remote or high-risk environments may suffer injuries or lose communication without immediate support. Delayed detection reduces survival chances and compromises mission effectiveness. The system must convert field data into actionable intelligence with low latency under limited bandwidth.

Research Objectives
  • Monitor vital signs in near real time
  • Detect fall events and emergency conditions quickly
  • Transmit alerts over long distances securely
  • Provide a simple wearable architecture for field use
  • Visualize soldier health and GPS via a live dashboard
  • Predict health risk and communication link risk using ML
Methodology

The project uses a two-unit wearable architecture. The wrist unit captures health data using MAX30102 and MLX90614 sensors. The shoulder unit performs fall detection via MPU6050, acquires GPS coordinates, and forwards integrated data through SX1278 LoRa to the monitoring side.

For visualization, a master ESP32 node forwards parsed data via Wi-Fi using MQTT/HTTP. Node-RED handles backend processing, and the dashboard presents live metrics, map tracking, and alert states. A prediction layer classifies health risk as Normal / Warning / Critical and link risk as Stable / Weak / High Risk.
